About me

Earth observation meets data intelligence

I am a PhD candidate at the Czech University of Life Sciences in Prague, specialising in agricultural drought vulnerability, remote sensing and climate risk modelling across the Danube River Basin — an 8-country transboundary system feeding over 80 million people.

My work combines satellite-derived climate indices (SPEI-3, ESI, SWI), geospatial analysis and machine learning to build decision-support tools for food and water security. My first-author paper, published in the International Journal of Climatology (Wiley, 2025), introduces an Integrated Drought Vulnerability Index validated across two decades of data.

Beyond academia I hold the Google AI Professional Certificate and am completing the IBM Machine Learning Professional Certificate — bridging research-grade earth science with industry-ready ML pipelines.

Actively seeking roles in EO, climate tech, AgTech and geospatial AI — globally, remote-first, open to relocation for the right team.

PhD Researcher

Oct 2022 - Present
Czech University of Life Sciences (CZU), Prague
  • Quantified agricultural drought vulnerability across an 8-country transboundary basin (Danube) using SPEI-3, ESI, SWI, and NDVI on 22 years of satellite data — integrated into a novel IDVI framework published in the International Journal of Climatology (2025).
  • Built a full supervised ML drought classification pipeline (Python · scikit-learn): Gradient Boosting classifier, F1 = 0.887, AUC = 0.960, across 16 meteorological stations and 60 years (11,452 observations); feature engineering included cyclical month encoding, lag variables, rolling precipitation means, and temperature anomalies.
  • Deployed unsupervised climate regime detection (PCA → DBSCAN): recovered north–south aridity gradient and post-2000 warming shift across Moldova without supervised labels; ARI = 0.098 — best of 6 competing models.
  • Developed ERA5 desktop extractor app (Python · customtkinter · Cartopy · netCDF4): handles 60,000+ row datasets with threaded loading, Cartopy contour maps, and CSV export.
  • Co-authored copula modelling study (C-vine copula, 278 districts) linking maize yield losses to multi-scale drought compound events — published in Agricultural Water Management (Elsevier, 2023).
